Nutrition for Your Results

Food guidance based on what was found in your document. These are general nutritional considerations — not a prescribed diet.

Because of borderline LDL

Eat more omega-3 rich foods — salmon, mackerel, sardines, walnuts, flaxseed. Omega-3 fatty acids are known to help maintain healthy LDL and HDL balance.

Increase soluble fibre — oats, barley, legumes (lentils, chickpeas), apples, and psyllium. Soluble fibre binds cholesterol in the digestive system and helps remove it from the body.

Reduce saturated fats — limit red meat, full-fat dairy, fried foods, and processed snacks. Saturated fat is associated with raising LDL levels.

Add plant sterols — found in fortified foods (some margarine, yoghurt). Plant sterols can help block cholesterol absorption.

Blood glucose is normal — maintain it

Eat complex carbohydrates — brown rice, sweet potato, whole grain bread. These digest slower and keep blood sugar stable throughout the day.

Space meals evenly — eating every 3–4 hours prevents blood sugar spikes and crashes, which helps maintain your current healthy glucose level.
